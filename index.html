A family business, built on the Rai Coast.

Dipsa Business Group started with a simple problem: farmers in Saidor and Nayudo were growing excellent coffee with nowhere reliable to sell it. We set out to fix that — and we're still fixing it, one buying trip at a time.

Why we exist

Coffee has always grown well in Saidor and Nayudo. What's been missing is a buyer farmers can actually count on — one that shows up on schedule, pays a fair price on the spot, and doesn't disappear after one bad season.

Dipsa Business Group was founded to be that buyer. We're a family-owned enterprise, incorporated under the PNG Business Groups Incorporation Act, and based directly in the communities we buy from — not a distant company sending agents through occasionally.

Our model is deliberately simple: buy parchment coffee directly from farmers at a fair, transparent price, carry the transport burden ourselves, and sell on to established exporters in Goroka. No unnecessary middlemen, no disappearing acts.

As we grow, we're investing what we earn back into the business — better storage, our own transport, and eventually grading and drying facilities that put more money in farmers' pockets, not less.

What we believe

Three things guide every buying trip, every price we set, and every decision about where to invest next.

①

Fair price, every time

The price we quote is the price we pay. No discounting after the scale reads, no excuses on collection day.

②

We carry the burden, not the farmer

Transport, logistics, and the cost of reaching remote villages are our problem to solve — not the farmer's.

③

Built to stay, not pass through

We live in this district. Being here next season — and the one after — is the whole point.

Running now, and what's next

Each service below is at a different stage — from fully operational to early planning. We'd rather be upfront about that than overstate where we are.

①

Parchment coffee buying

Direct purchase from farmers in Saidor and Nayudo at transparent, competitive prices. Every batch is inspected on the spot before purchase, and payment happens at the point of collection — not weeks later.

Running now
②

Transport & logistics

Collection by dinghy across river and coastal routes, and by truck for the Saidor–Goroka corridor. Farmers don't need to find their own way to a buyer — we come to the village.

Running now
③

Coffee grading

On-site grading to assess and categorise coffee quality, helping farmers understand exactly what drives a better price — and how to get there next harvest.

In development
④

Drying facilities

Proper drying infrastructure at our Saidor depot to cut post-harvest losses and protect coffee quality during the wait for transport.

In development
⑤

Packaging & branding

Quality, recognisable packaging so Rai Coast coffee is identified — and priced — as a premium product, not a generic commodity.

In development
⑥

Field agent network

Training and employing local youth as buying agents, so we can reach more villages across Rai Coast without losing the direct, personal relationship farmers trust us for.

In development

The long-term goal: process parchment into green bean ourselves and access export markets directly, capturing more of the value chain for the business and the farmers in it.
